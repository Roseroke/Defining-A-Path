               <section id="part1" class="mb-16">
                   <h2 class="text-4xl font-bold text-slate-800 border-b-2 border-slate-300 pb-2 mb-8">Part I: Diagnosis of the Current System</h2>
                   
                   <article id="introduction" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">Introduction</h3>
                       <div class="space-y-4 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                           <p>My name is Roosevelt. I do not believe I come equipped with anything extraordinary that allows me to formulate ideas; I am one of many. I have always felt like I was never taught anything of importance, and that desire for knowledge has led me on a journey to compile a solution and path forward for not just America but all of humanity as new technologies begin to change our perception of work, money, and time. The undertaking of being critical of an entity like the United States in its entirety leaves the undisciplined prone to generalizations. My goal is to shed light on the nuances of American Society and to communicate these ideas in a way that makes sense while instilling the ideal of meaning and purpose. If anything sticks with you, let it be this: The United States Government is not just one person but an entire set of moving parts, people, and institutions working together.</p>
                           <p>In our current system, those willing to engage in acts of selflessness often receive nothing for their good nature and are instead sought out and exploited. This system is dominated by capitalistic concepts where success is relative to one's ability to cultivate a relevant skill, leading to niches in the job market. In my opinion, the numerous "Specialized jobs" amount to nothing but cacophonous busy work made to drown the human "spirit".</p>
                       </div>
                   </article>

                   <article id="obfuscation" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">1. On Government, Exploitation, and Obfuscation</h3>
                       <div class="space-y-6 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">1.1 Redefining the Role of Government</h4>
                               <p>I define government as an entity in which a population can entrust its wellbeing. A fundamental function of an elected government is to determine its own course to achieve the people's mandate. We operate as a representative democracy, where elected officials are paid to use their powers to make agencies perform pertinent actions for the community. The issue enters with Special Interest Groups, usually partnered with rich corporations or governments, that have an interest in a politician uniquely positioned to financially aid them.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">1.2 The Mechanics of Exploitation</h4>
                               <p>The ends do not justify the means. To reach a utopian world devoid of hunger and war, those with the vision will, without remorse, exploit the less fortunate. The best way to ensure their continued wealth is to make everyone "the less fortunate". Mankind has long exploited one another to enhance personal wealth. The only difference now is that we have become more clever and cunning, able to use ignorance and obfuscation as a smokescreen.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">1.3 The Primary Tool: Obfuscation</h4>
                               <p>To "obfuscate" is to make something obscure, unclear, or unintelligible. In our society, several "sieves" exist in the job and recruitment sector, strengthened by obfuscation: Etiquette/Adaptability, Culture, Time: Pay Ratio, Social Intelligence/IQ, and Previous Experience. This terminology barrier is evident in fields like finance and law. Complex tax laws and insurance policies use terminology that bars those with less agency from understanding systems that directly impact them. This extends to legislators, who use complex and convoluted wording in laws that can be interpreted loosely to give power to those who already have it.</p>
                           </div>
                       </div>
                   </article>
                   
                   <article id="critique" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">2. Critique of Core Societal Pillars</h3>
                        <div class="space-y-6 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">2.1 The Economy</h4>
                               <p>There is a fundamental lack of direction in the United States. The current economic system is untenable. Those who benefited from the post-WWII boom are now in control, leaving the youth with a nonsensical economy manipulated by those with the assets to do so. Those that can make money by making up rules—through hedge funds, trusts, etc.—are "Sophist Thieves," who benefit from the activities of real criminals. The more crime plagues a country, the richer they all grow. The Federal Reserve is purely reactionary, generating policy to either recoup losses or kick the can down the road.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">2.2 Education</h4>
                               <p>The US education system leaves the youth feeling lost or apathetic. Social media has shifted focus to identity and assets over character. Instead of teaching practical skills like economics or taxes, education dilutes the job pool, leaving youth to fall into their socioeconomic place in retail or fast food. Higher education is gated by obscene paywalls, leading to massive debt. My goal is to allocate youth into stable, futuristic industries from a young age to guide their action and drive.</p>
                           </div>
                       </div>
                   </article>
               </section>

               <!-- Part II: The Proposed Solution -->
               <section id="part2" class="mb-16">
                   <h2 class="text-4xl font-bold text-slate-800 border-b-2 border-slate-300 pb-2 mb-8">Part II: The Proposed Solution</h2>

                   <article id="principles" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">1. Core Principles of the New System</h3>
                       <div class="space-y-6 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">1.1 A New Philosophy: Collective Servitism</h4>
                               <p>This proposal is built on **Collective Servitism**: an ethical shift towards service contribution beyond self-interest as the primary measure of value. The society proposed is one where all participants engage in daily actions to pursue a higher goal: the ascendancy of humanity into a space-faring cosmic race. This process must be equitable for all.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">1.2 A New Definition: Work Ethic</h4>
                               <p>Work Ethic is redefined as: "The ability of the individual to perform work and or tasks in service of a goal beyond self-enrichment". Access to higher-tiered rewards is directly related to this ethic. The goal is to reward good, punish evil, foster love, ostracize hate, incentivize selflessness, and punish exploitation.</p>
                           </div>
                       </div>
                   </article>
                   
                   <article id="uc" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">2. The Engine of Change: The Universal Catalogue (UC)</h3>
                       <div class="space-y-6 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                            <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">2.1 Concept and Function</h4>
                               <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2">
                                   <li><b>What is it?</b> A comprehensive, secure, and always-updating digital catalogue of commodities, goods, and services.</li>
                                   <li><b>How does one earn credits?</b> By performing societally beneficial tasks relative to one's capabilities.</li>
                                   <li><b>Are Credits just another form of Money?</b> In a sense, but they are a regulated currency exclusive to the Catalogue and cannot be exchanged for other currencies.</li>
                                   <li><b>Can Credits be Traded?</b> Yes, but only in person and initiated by the giver.</li>
                               </ul>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">2.2 Phased Implementation: The Experiments</h4>
                               <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2">
                                   <li><b>Experiment Alpha:</b> Create a network of warehouses to store items for the UC.</li>
                                   <li><b>Experiment Beta:</b> Incentivize learning in schools by distributing Credits for task completion.</li>
                                   <li><b>Experiment Omega:</b> Establish "embassies" for citizens to get daily job contracts, earning Credits and investment dollars.</li>
                                   <li><b>Experiment Delta:</b> Provide licensed electric scooters to facilitate transportation.</li>
                                   <li><b>Experiment Gamma:</b> Create "Embassilic Cells" by redeveloping housing and creating local, self-sustaining service hubs.</li>
                               </ul>
                           </div>
                       </div>
                   </article>

                   <article id="agencies" class="mb-12">

                       <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700 mb-6">These government institutions will provide baseline support. The following departments will be created, divided by function:</p>

                   <article id="reforming" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">4. Reforming Key Institutions</h3>
                        <div class="space-y-6 text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">4.1 Culture of the Unailed Weak</h4>
                               <p>There will be those physically capable but unable to work due to upbringing or other factors. By virtue of humanity, no one can be denied a place. They will be allocated according to an analysis of their capability and desire to reach transcendent selflessness.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">4.2 Armed Forces and Police</h4>
                               <p>The desire to protect one's country should stem from a need to defend against weakness, not from monetary incentive. While existing military structures are operationally proficient, their cultures need evaluation. A centralized national police force is proposed to enhance coordination on inter-state crime.</p>
                           </div>
                           <div>
                               <h4 class="text-2xl font-medium text-slate-600 mb-2">4.3 Prison and Gangs</h4>
                               <p>There is a need to isolate bad actors, but also a path towards rehabilitation for those who want to reintegrate. The massive prison population is a testament to societal failure. However, there can be no tolerance for enemies of the citizenry who disrupt public safety.</p>
                           </div>
                       </div>

                   <article id="contention" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">1. Contention: Privatization and Competition</h3>
                       <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">This system is not an all-consuming monolith. The solution is baseline competition. The proposed agencies will operate as government-run, publicly traded businesses. It is not my intention to remove private ownership; rather, the government will compete with private enterprise to incentivize innovation. The goal is to diminish exploitation while strengthening individual liberty, autonomy, and purpose.</p>
                   </article>

                   <article id="authoritarianism" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">2. Authoritarianism and Big Brother</h3>
                       <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">New ideas bring skepticism. The fear is that humans are fundamentally selfish. However, the society I propose is a constitutional democracy where the rule of law is paramount. Due process, individual civil liberties, federal elections, and term limits will remain unchanged in their overall mandate.</p>
                   </article>

                   <article id="final-conclusion" class="mb-12">
                       <h3 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-slate-700 mb-4">3. Conclusion: On Money and Motivation</h3>
                       <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-700">I have concluded that money itself is not the problem. It has become a bastardization of wealth, manifesting as frivolous spending and nepotistic consolidation. Money exists as a vassal through which "Control" can manifest. The problem is fundamental and continues as long as "self and now" is valued above all else. This proposal seeks to define a new path forward.</p>
